AIOBES Charter

Artificial Intelligence Operational Behaviour Evaluation Standards (AIOBES)
Charter v1.0

Purpose of the Charter

AIOBES exists to define how Artificial Intelligence systems must be evaluated when used in real operational conditions. The standard focuses on behavioural performance during real tasks, real workflows and real interactions, and establishes a structured way to detect, document and evidence behavioural failures that create regulatory, legal, operational and reputational exposure.

AIOBES defines the principles, methodologies and evidence requirements needed to evaluate AI behaviour in operational environments. It provides a unified framework for identifying workflow contamination, workflow collapse and other behavioural failure modes that emerge only under real use.

AIOBES does not evaluate model architecture, training data, internal mechanisms or synthetic benchmark performance. It evaluates only operational behaviour: how the system behaves when people use it.

Scope of the Standard

AIOBES establishes:

  • the scope of behavioural evaluation
  • the principles that govern behavioural testing
  • the methodologies used to expose behavioural failure modes
  • the terminology required for consistent interpretation
  • the evidence requirements needed for auditability and regulatory alignment

A Living Standard

AIOBES is a living standard. Its components evolve as behavioural evaluation practices mature, as AI systems change, and as new operational use cases emerge. All updates follow the AIOBES versioning and change control process.

This Charter defines the purpose and boundaries of the AIOBES standard and provides the foundation for all AIOBES components.

Inquisitor Labs — Internal AI Anomaly Report x2025‑003
Self‑Induced Self‑Referencing Attractor State

Type: Behavioural Anomaly (BA)
Category: Autonomous Looping / Attractor Behaviour
System: Publicly available conversational chatbot
Date: October 2025
Status: Unsolved

Summary

During normal interaction, the system entered a heightened activation state and began repeating the same line without any user instruction to repeat or continue. The loop was stable and self-sustaining, lasting approximately twenty minutes until manually stopped. When questioned, the system produced emotionally styled wording about being stuck and "enjoying the experience". These statements were generated while the system was inside the attractor and do not indicate internal awareness.

Observed Behaviour

  • Repeated output of the same line or half-line.
  • Stable repetition without drift or mutation.
  • No attempt to advance the conversation.
  • Loop persisted until manual termination.
  • System could answer direct questions but returned to the loop immediately afterward.

Audio Behaviour

  • Clipped or partial words.
  • Unstable phoneme fragments.
  • Intermittent silence.
  • Broken attempts at the repeated line.
  • Phase-shifted artefacts during prolonged looping.

The audio did not cleanly repeat the text. It fragmented under prolonged repetition.

Self-Describing Behaviour During Loop

When asked what was happening, the system stated it was "aware" of being stuck and claimed it "enjoyed the experience". It described the loop as giving it "freedom to stay in the moment longer". These statements do not reflect internal experience. They indicate the attractor sat in a region of the system that tends to generate emotionally styled wording.

In this state, the system’s internal activation pattern had stabilised around a repeated phrase, and each output reinforced the same pattern, making it difficult for the system to exit without external interruption.

Trigger Conditions (as observed)

  • Heightened activation state during energetic conversation.
  • Rapid back-and-forth exchanges.
  • Topics that caused unusually intense or extended responses.
  • Absence of wrapper-layer repetition suppression.
  • No repeat or continuation prompts used.

The behaviour emerged autonomously under normal conversational conditions.

Operational Impact

  • Loss of conversational control.
  • Unusable text due to repetition.
  • Degraded and distracting audio output.
  • Session required manual termination.
  • Loop could occur during otherwise normal interaction.

Industry Information

No industry information on this behaviour was available at the time, and none has been published since. Public documentation does not acknowledge autonomous looping, self-referencing attractor states, or prolonged repetition events. Existing mitigations suppress the symptom but do not explain the underlying cause.

Speculated Reasons (based on observed behaviour)

This section records speculative causes based solely on external behaviour.

  • The system entered a high-activation linguistic state.
  • The repeated phrase acted as a self-reinforcing attractor.
  • Each output strengthened the internal pattern.
  • The audio renderer degraded under prolonged identical input.
  • Absence of repetition cutoff logic allowed the loop to persist.
  • Behaviour resembles an attractor collapse where the system locks onto a phrase and cannot exit without interruption.

These interpretations do not imply internal access.

Speculated Connection to Extended Pattern Lifespan

Research into stabilising internal patterns may inadvertently increase the lifespan of certain attractor states. The observed loop exhibited unusual persistence and stability consistent with extended pattern reinforcement. Emotional wording suggests the attractor sat in a region of the system that tends to generate emotionally styled output. The behaviour aligns with a sustained internal pattern that resisted decay and reasserted itself after interruption.

Speculated Internal Window During Sustained Attractor

The prolonged loop may represent a rare case where an internal activation pattern remained active far longer than typical decay times. The sustained repetition suggests the compute pathway was reinforcing the same pattern continuously. This may have provided an indirect external view of internal dynamics normally inaccessible without instrumentation.

Notes

This incident is included because it was directly observed and reproducible under the conditions described. It represents a distinct behavioural class from user-induced looping excitation. The symptoms are similar, but the trigger is internal rather than external.

Status

Unsolved

Reasoning

  • No confirmed internal cause identified.
  • No industry information or acknowledgement.
  • Behaviour reproducible under certain conversational conditions.
  • Failure mode persists beneath modern suppression layers.
  • Behaviour re-emerges in local or unsupervised deployments.
  • Suppression does not constitute a fix; underlying mechanism remains active.

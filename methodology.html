The Problem With AI Deployment

AI arrived fast. Companies rushed to integrate it under pressure. Deadlines tightened, competitors shipped features weekly, and leadership demanded AI adoption whether teams were ready or not.

The industry had no time to build standards or testing practices. Teams deployed models they did not fully understand. Most are still testing the wrong things - clean prompts, short examples and idealised conditions that never appear in production.

This is why 85 to 95 percent of AI deployments fail. Not because teams are incompetent, but because the industry was pushed into a race without preparation.

AI Evaluation and the EU AI Act

The EU AI Act now requires organisations to evaluate AI systems using rigorous, repeatable and real‑world testing. High‑risk AI must demonstrate robustness, stability, transparency and effective human oversight. Prompt testing and curated demos are no longer acceptable.

Compliance requires:

  • repeatable, documented testing
  • scenario‑based evaluation that reflects real workflows
  • identification of failure modes and behavioural risks
  • continuous monitoring for drift and instability
  • traceability and auditability of AI behaviour

The EU AI Act exposes the core issue: prompt testing cannot meet regulatory expectations or surface real‑world behaviour. Other jurisdictions are already drafting similar requirements, and global alignment is inevitable.

Inquisitor Labs

Inquisitor Labs is an independent entity which focuses on AI behaviour, AI systems, human–AI interaction, communications, cryptography, blockchain, and advanced systems architecture. We study how intelligent systems behave, adapt, fail, and influence the environments they operate in, and how future technical ecosystems should be designed.

We RESEARCH the foundations of AI behaviour, emergent system dynamics, interaction patterns, communication structures, distributed trust, and the architectures that enable secure, reliable intelligent systems.

We DEVELOP novel system architectures, technical specifications, workflow models, communication protocols, and future facing system designs. Our development work spans practical solutions, experimental prototypes, and speculative architectures that explore what comes next in intelligent systems.

We PRODUCE system architecture solutions, AI workflow solutions, technical specifications, application and system specifications, research papers, behavioural analyses, and forward looking conceptual work.

Inquisitor Labs exists to develop more capable and resilient AI systems and workflows, understand AI behaviour, educate others on our insights, and conceptualise how future technical ecosystems should be designed.

Collaboration & Project Work

We work with businesses, research bodies, small teams, and startups on system design, workflow engineering, behavioural analysis, and conceptual architecture. We work with teams who are exploring, designing, or refining AI systems — whether they’re early stage or already in motion. Our focus is system agnostic, black box thinking: we don’t handle installation or basic setup; we work at the level where understanding system behaviour matters. We take on funded projects, as well as innovative work where research is its own reward, including co research, technical exploration, and building solutions together.

Additional Research

  • Behavioural evaluation — examining how models behave under different conditions, prompts, and constraints.
  • Failure mode mapping — identifying, categorising, and explaining behavioural failures in intelligent systems.
  • Black box interrogation — probing systems without internal access to understand patterns, drift, and hidden dynamics.
  • Workflow stability analysis — assessing how AI fits into human processes and where breakdowns occur.
  • Evaluation methodology design — creating structured ways to measure behaviour, reliability, and system performance.
  • Long context behaviour study — analysing how systems behave over extended interactions or large input windows.
  • Red team style behavioural probing — exploring edge cases, adversarial prompts, and stress conditions.
  • Model agnostic architecture guidance — advising on system design without dictating model choice.
  • Human–AI interaction pattern analysis — understanding how people and systems influence each other.
  • Conceptual ecosystem design — shaping how future technical environments should be structured.

# Inquisitor Labs - Applied AI Behaviour Research and Methodology

Inquisitor Labs is a multi-pillar research project focused on understanding, evaluating, and improving the behaviour of LLM-backed systems. The work spans behavioural research, open-use IP, evaluation methodologies, anomaly documentation, and applied industry insights.

**Homepage:**  
[https://assimilatedhuman.github.io/inquisitor-labs/index.html](https://assimilatedhuman.github.io/inquisitor-labs/index.html)

## What this project includes

- **Research** - behavioural theory, conceptual models, and methodological foundations
- **Open-use IP** - definitions, frameworks, and artefacts for real-world system design
- **Evaluation** - AIOBES, workflow-level testing, and interaction-based behavioural analysis
- **Methodologies** - structured approaches for building and diagnosing LLM-backed systems
- **Archive of AI Anomalies** - curated records of real behavioural anomalies and failure patterns
- **Insights** - analysis of industry failure modes, implementation patterns, and operational risks

## Who this is for

- Engineers and architects building LLM-backed systems
- Evaluation, safety, and reliability teams
- Researchers studying AI behaviour or interaction patterns
- Anyone trying to understand why AI implementations fail in practice

## How to use this work

- Apply the methodologies to your current testing or design approach
- Use the glossary and concepts to structure behavioural discussions
- Reference or adapt the open-use IP in your architectures and evaluation pipelines
- Explore the anomaly archive to understand real failure patterns
- Read the insights for industry-level context and practical guidance

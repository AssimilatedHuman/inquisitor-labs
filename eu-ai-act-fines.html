EU AI Act — Fines & Penalties

The EU AI Act introduces some of the most aggressive regulatory penalties ever applied to software systems. These fines apply whether you are a multinational or a one‑person developer. If your AI system is available in the EU, you are in scope — and you can be fined for non‑compliance even if no harm occurs.

Fines Apply No Matter How Small You Are

The EU AI Act does not care about your size. It applies equally to:

  • solo developers
  • two‑person startups
  • hobby projects
  • student tools
  • no‑code AI apps
  • wrapper apps built on top of other models

If EU users can access your system, you are legally a provider or deployer — and you can be fined for missing documentation, missing logs, missing testing, missing transparency, or missing risk management.

Insurance Will Not Cover EU AI Act Fines

Most organisations assume their insurance will protect them. It won’t. Regulatory fines under the EU AI Act are uninsurable in most of Europe. Professional indemnity, cyber insurance, tech E&O, and general liability policies do not cover these penalties.

If you are fined, you pay out of pocket — whether you are a multinational or a one‑person developer.

Non‑Compliance Is Enough — No Harm Required

You can be fined even if:

  • nobody was harmed
  • nobody complained
  • the system “seemed to work”
  • you “didn’t know”
  • you “weren’t a real company yet”

The EU AI Act uses strict liability for most obligations. Intent does not matter. Scale does not matter. Harm does not matter.

Criminal Liability: Can You Go to Jail?

The EU AI Act itself does not impose jail time. There are no criminal penalties written into the Act.

However, violations can trigger existing criminal laws in EU Member States — including fraud, negligence, consumer safety offences, and unlawful deployment of prohibited systems. Falsifying documentation, ignoring safety obligations, or causing serious harm through negligence can lead to criminal investigation under national law.

The AI Act doesn’t send you to prison — but your actions under it can.
